Linear Function Transformation and Domain Explanation

3. Transformation Explanation

The function y = 0.5x + 5 models the design of a children's straight slide. Starting from the parent function y = x, which has a slope of 1 and represents a 45° incline, two key transformations are applied to achieve a safe and realistic model suitable for young children.

  • Slope Adjustment (Reducing Steepness): The slope is reduced from 1 to 0.5, meaning that for every unit of horizontal movement, the vertical change is only half as much. This vertical compression reduces the angle of inclination from 45° to approximately 26.56°, creating a gentler slope that meets safety standards for children's slides, ensuring controlled speed and safety during descent.
  • Intercept Setting (Starting Height): The y-intercept is set at 5 (in decimeters), which raises the entire function upward by 5 units. This represents the starting height of the slide at 5 dm, a height that allows easy access for children while maintaining enjoyable sliding dynamics suitable for ages 3–6.

These transformations together yield a practical slide model that reflects the essential characteristics of a safe, smooth, and fun playground design.

4. Domain and Range Explanation

  • Domain: The horizontal length of the slide must remain within a safe and comfortable range for children. Therefore, x ∈ [0, 20] (in decimeters) represents the slide's total horizontal distance of 20 dm, covering the full sliding path from top to bottom.
  • Range: From y = 0.5x + 5, when x = 0, y = 5 (the starting height); and when x = 20, y = 15 (the end height, near ground level). Thus, the range is y ∈ [5, 15] (in decimeters), which ensures a sufficient height difference while keeping the slide's end safely close to the ground.

The function y = 0.5x + 5 accurately models the gentle incline of a children's straight slide, combining appropriate slope and height to meet safety and enjoyment requirements.
